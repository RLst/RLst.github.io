About Me

Passionate. Perfectionist. Programmer.

Hi and welcome! I'm Tony Le! I am a game programmer that is currently studying for an Advanced Diploma of Professional Game Development at AIE (Academy of Interactive Entertainment). I have successfully completed the first year with successful results and look forward to my second year at AIE.

My journey into programming started at the age of 15 when I bought a PC magazine that came with a cover CD bundled with a program called DarkBASIC. This was essentially a framework or system for making games, and it came with it's own IDE and compiler. It had an easy to use language that was specifically designed with very game specific commands. 

The process of programing was procedural and logical which I found rather fun and mentally engaging. And the idea that I could make my own games was mind blowing. This was definitely something I wanted to be a part of in my near future.

Programming is something I do with enthusiasm, drive and commitment. I think that it is fundamental for code to run as efficiently as possible, not be wasteful of system resources while at the same time be clean and readable.

I've written a number of apps both minor and major for my assessments at AIE, as well as having a number of personal projects I'm currently working on on the side. I plan to one day release a few games on the app store and potentially start my game studio.

My belief is that you should always be striving to be your best and that means constantly improving yourself by actively learning and expanding your skill sets.

I'm currently studying full time and am actively seeking work in the industry. In my spare time you'll find me working on DIY projects at home, enjoying a game of tennis with friends and family or exploring local areas nearby.

Feel free to contact me.
